Bachelor of Social Work

A Bachelor of Social Work (BSW) degree program equips students with the knowledge and skills to address societal issues, promote social justice, and improve the well-being of individuals, families, and communities:
  • Curriculum
    • BSW programs typically include a blend of theory and practice courses, such as community development, social administration, and evaluation research. Students may also complete field work placement exercises.
  • Career opportunities
    • Graduates can work in a variety of settings, including family service centers, children and youth centers, hospitals, hospices, and other community or social service agencies.
  • Job outlook
    • The social work field is expected to grow by 9% from 2021 to 2031. Accrediting body The Council on Social Work Education (CSWE) is the national accrediting body for all bachelor's and master's level social work programs. Some roles of a social worker include:
    Client assessment Developing and implementing treatment plans ,Securing and facilitating access to resources ,Monitoring and evaluating progress, Client advocacy, Empathy and active listening, Respect for individuality and Building trust
